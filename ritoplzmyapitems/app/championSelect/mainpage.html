Will of the Ancients

Will of the Ancients was changed so it no longer has spell vamp, but you heal for 15% of the damage dealt, calculated before your opponent's resistances. While this is a minor nerf to killing minions, it is a buff against champions with ~50 MR. How do these changes balance out? Is it worth purchasing?


This means you'll be spell vamping for less, but you won't need as much (high base damage, resistance penetration, etc) to get your heal ball rolling. Riot in Patch 5.13 Notes

Rylai's and Liandry's

Rylai's: the new permaslow. The synergy is real here... or is it only for Kog'maw?


With just the Liandry's under a champion's belt, any damage-over-time effects deal their normal damage plus 2% of a target's current health per second. But when that target is under a movement-impairing effect, that damage is doubled. Can you guess what grants all area of effect spells the ability to impair movement? That's right, Rylai's Crystal Scepter.

The combination of the two is downright brutal on just about any mage with big AOE spells that happen to apply a DoT.

Taylor "Riot RoboTayCo" Cocke in The Magical AP Item Changes with the Unicorns of Love
